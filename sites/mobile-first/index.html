Situation

Care teams were contending with highly variable and manual clinical processes, causing persistent overcrowding, delays in care and inaccurate discharge dates. The hospital was looking to coordinate the delivery of care to provide an optimal patient experience.

Solution

100% compliance in the first week of rollout.

A comprehensive care management platform streamlines the patient care process and providing everyone on a patient’s care team extensive patient information, collaborative tools, and hospital resource availability, wherever they need it – in real time.

Result

The new platform saves physicians an average of 2 hours per day, achieving a break-even point in less than 3 months.

With the platform coordinating processes and paperwork, care teams can more easily collaborate and focus on their patients, leading to healthier outcomes.

The problem

The hospital was admitting a growing number of patients with complex symptoms. Patients with complex conditions require care from multiple departments. These departments need to communicate and collaborate effectively inorder to deliver care. The hospital brought in Dale Potter as Chief Infomation officer. With Potter’s background in manufacturing, he was suprised to find wide variation of manual processes in the hospital.

How does high variation in process and delayed discharge dates affect patient care?

The approach

Potter grounded the approach on a simple premise: listen to the clinicians; lead with user needs vs. technology. In order to do this, they needed to understand the care process and standardize it.

"We're drawing what people do naturally, out of their heads, looking at best practices, trying to meld those together to be best practice."

-Dale Potter
