Mobility means immediacy

Boosting employee productivity with mobile means customers get a faster response.

Whether it's a doctor attending to patients or an employee talking with customers, mobile communication saves time and boosts enterprise efficiency.

Expand to see how

Using its mobile-enabled care-delivery platform, The Ottawa Hospital's physicians saved as much as two hours a day, by sharing information faster, collaborating better, and performing fewer manual and administrative tasks. That meant two more hours every day to spend with patients and their families at the bedside, contributing to a better patient experience.

“

When we brought the mobile devices in and had direct access to the information at the bedside, we would say, this is your result…and that changed how they thought about their disease.”

—Dr. Glen Geiger, Chief Medical Information Officer, The Ottawa Hospital

Mobility means efficiency

Introducing mobile devices to an enterprise can effectively end wasteful delegation of simple tasks.

One ongoing challenge for The Ottawa Hospital was a tedious regulatory administrative task that hindered industry compliance.

Expand to see how

Computerized physician order entry (CPOE) required doctors to jot handwritten instructions for a colleague to transcribe and enter into a data platform.

But implementing IBM mobile technology into the hospital's infrastructure turned CPOE into an automated task that the physicians now execute instantly, from anywhere.

The Ottawa Hospital's physicians manage nearly 90 percent of patient orders, up from 20 percent in 2009, using mobile devices.

“

[Physicians now say,] 'I can hit the submit button, walk out of the room and I don't have to remember to give it to somebody to do.'”

—Dale Potter, SVP–Strategy and Transformation, The Ottawa Hospital

Mobile means results

Using a mobile platform can help an enterprise build revenue through greater efficiency at a lower cost.

IBM mobile technology has helped The Ottawa Hospital grow its revenue through efficient, cost-saving processes.

Expand to see how

Spotlight on security

Banks secure their customers' sensitive information for mobile infrastructures—so a hospital can do the same for its patients.

More than seven out of 10 IT managers regard security as the greatest challenge of integrating mobility into their platforms.

Expand to see how

But technology leaders at The Ottawa Hospital use IBM tools to govern and secure their mobile enterprise for compliance around patient confidentiality.

Patient names are excluded from tablet notifications, and individual information is stored only in the cloud, not locally on devices. And the cache of patient information is cleared every time the application is used.

Six components of mobile success

Whether expanding mobile efforts or just starting out, every enterprise needs to examine several key considerations.

Expand to see how

An enterprise just starting in mobile should:

  • Consider where and how customer interactions take place
  • Identify opportunities for more effective mobile delivery
  • Incorporate mobility into the existing security infrastructure

For an organization with an established mobile platform, key tactics include:

  • Developing a mobile API portfolio and management strategy
  • Increasing mobile engagement with emerging cloud services
  • Using analytics to help lead customers to new revenue and new business
